Original Intention of Design

In today's digital landscape, where the internet serves as the primary source of news and information, misinformation and fake news have become increasingly prevalent. This project introduces a browser extension prototype designed specifically for Google Chrome to help users identify and avoid unreliable content, encouraging the consumption of fact-based and relatively objective news articles and publications.

By leveraging artificial intelligence, this extension evaluates news reliability through multiple dimensions, such as source credibility, content accuracy, logical consistency, and content style, providing real-time feedback to users on the trustworthiness of the articles they read (including article credibility scores and explanations for each dimension). In addition, the extension can generate long-term statistics for analytics and show users how consumption patterns and opinions change over time.

This innovative solution addresses a critical problem in the modern media ecosystem: the proliferation of misinformation. It empowers users, especially university students, to navigate the complex information environment more discerningly. Research indicates that nearly 99.7% of students engage with news regularly, highlighting the need for tools that help them critically assess the information they consume. By promoting awareness of how online content shapes opinions and providing insights to help users make more informed choices, this extension aims to combat misinformation effectively while fostering a more discerning, informed readership.
